Centre of Excellence

The government’s vision is to support further development of Centres of Excellence in adoption services.

To turn this vision in to practical reality, a 2-year period of funding is made available for Regional Adoption Agencies to develop multidisciplinary / multiagency approaches to provide better services for adopted

children, young people, and their families and to secure permanent, sustainable funding arrangements in partnership with local authorities and health commissioners.

Development of Centres of Excellence in Adoption

Project Board - Terms of Reference

The Project Board for the Developing Centres of Excellence in Adoption programme, representing key stakeholders and experts by experience, is set to support the delivery of the overall CofE Programme. The board gives an oversight for the programme by reviewing the regional projects and the overall programme’s progress against plans. The board helps the programme and the regional projects by giving strategic direction and support when required. The project board reviews and makes decisions of any requests that materially change the original project plans. The Programme Board has a key role in supporting the National Adoption Strategic Lead and the RAA Leaders Group in making decisions and providing both challenge and approval on issues affecting the progress of the programme.

The board and its members create and maintain effective communication links between the programme / projects and organisations and stakeholder groups to ensure the dissemination of information and learning about the programme. The board provides advice and support for the academic partner who will be appointed to undertake research and evaluation of the previously funded multi-disciplinary teams and to look at themes emerging from the current projects. The Programme Board shall have no formal decision-making powers.

Membership

Membership of the board represents the different stakeholders within the adoption sector. On occasions, other individuals may be invited to attend meetings to provide specific information to support the Project Board’s decision-making.

Board members should be able to:

  • Understand programme and project plans
  • Own and communicate the programme vision
  • Broker relationships with stakeholders within and outside the programme
  • Be aware of the broader perspective and how it affects the programme
